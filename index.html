Preventive Dentistry

Never wait for a dental problem. However, well you brush your teeth there are surfaces of the teeth that you cannot clean effectively. At regular check-up exams, prevent them from happening.

Gum Health

When regular cleaning of teeth is not done and oral hygiene is ignored they lead to bacteria growing in number. Other factors increase that increase the susceptibility to gum disease include genetics and stress.

Root Canal

A root canal is a treatment used to repair and save a tooth that is badly decayed and pulp is infected. During a root canal procedure, the tooth nerves are removed and the inside of the tooth is cleaned and sealed.

Dental Fillings

Minor cavities can be restored with white (tooth-colored) filling. This type of treatment is a good way of correcting a small problem before it becomes a bigger and more expensive problem.

Crowns and Bridges

Dental crowns and bridges offer a way to protect weak, damaged and missing teeth while providing an aesthetic improvement. By combining function with esthetics, our team works with you to create attractive and functional dental restorations.

Extractions

Even though it is always preferable to preserve natural teeth, sometimes it is necessary to remove the teeth that are grossly decayed mobile teeth with periodontal problems, misaligned tooth also known as wisdom teeth.

Esthetic Dentistry

A Hollywood Smile Makeover or simply a Smile design is the process of improving the appearance of your smile through one or more cosmetic dental procedures, such as veneers, crowns, composite bonding, dental implants and teeth whitening.

Child Dentistry

Parents often tend to disregard the oral health and hygiene of the milk teeth assuming that these teeth will go away once the permanent teeth come. The pediatric dentist may schedule additional visits for children who are susceptible to tooth decay .

Dental Braces

Straight teeth contribute to healthy teeth and gums. Properly aligned teeth and jaws may alleviate or prevent physical health problems. Teeth that work better also tend to look better.

Dentures

When a tooth is lost, it affects the function of chewing and reduces our health in course of time. One missing tooth further increases the load on the remaining healthy teeth and weaken them too in time.

Dental Implants

Dental implants are the present most advantageous solution to replace lost tooth. They offer an excellent fixed (non-removable) solution to replace a single tooth or even many teeth that have been lost.

Scaling and Polishing

To ensure a beautiful healthy smile for life, we must brush our teeth twice a day. However, overtime, it leads to the building-up of plaque and hard deposits called tartar or calculus.

Invisalign

Invisalign uses virtually invisible aligners that you wear over your teeth. These clear aligners are custom-made for you and will gently and gradually shift your teeth in place. Invisalign aligners have no metal brackets or wires that need to be tightened, they are removable and can help you achieve a great smile without having to change your lifestyle.

Incognito Lingual Braces

Incognito braces are applied to the back of your teeth so no one will know you’re wearing braces. These “hidden” braces are customized with intelligent wires and brackets designed to deliver the individualized results your teeth need. Incognito braces are effective in fixing all types of cases for both children and adult.

Damon Braces

The Damon braces uses "self-ligating" braces that are designed to be discreet, comfortable and easy to keep clean. Damon braces has brackets that hold the wire in place without the need for elastics or metal ties, thus allowing for more freedom of movement and eliminating the need for tightening.

Ceramic Braces

Ceramic braces are made of strong composite materials that are made to match the color of the teeth. They are less noticeable making them a popular choice among many patients. The brackets are made of ceramic or porcelain materials and the wires are made of metal alloys and deliver a constant, gentle force to move teeth.

Metal Braces

Metal braces are the more traditional treatment option for correcting bites and crooked teeth. Strong, stainless steel brackets, tiny rubber bands called "ligatures" or "o-rings" and wires make up these braces. Metal brackets are less expensive than other bracket types. Patients are also offered a variety of choices when it comes to the colors of the ligatures.
